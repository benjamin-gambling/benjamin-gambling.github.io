About Me


I’m Ben and I’m a self-taught web developer currently living in Whistler, Canada with my partner. As you can probably tell by my choice of home, I love the outdoors, when I’m not working you’ll find me skiing, hiking, camping or discovering new places. When I have to be indoors you’ll find me watching sports or playing boards games like Settlers of Catan (look it up and thank me later).

Originally from Sheffield in the north of England, where I lived and studied until the age of 21 before making the move south to London after graduating from university. A couple of years down the line I came to the realization that i wanted to spend last time commuting and more time doing the things I love. So I decided to make a slightly bigger move to Australia where if you can believe it or not I found a love for skiing. Not wanting winter to end I decided to chase it all the way to Canada and i've never left since.

At the beginning of this year I decided to follow my heart and set out to become full time Web Developer. So the past few months it has been like a full time job, spending 40 hours a week following a few different curriculums and building projects that interested me and helped me cement what I had learnt.

I’m currently still learning and I doubt my hunger to learn more will stop anytime soon but I’ve reached a point that I feel I’m ready for the next step, to break into the industry and write code that actually helps people!

If you are new to web development or interested about my journey, keep an eye out for my blog, where I plan to document my journey from the very start through to my search for jobs, hopefully helping people in the same position that i was in all those months ago.

The University of Sheffield - MEng Civil & Structural Engineering
During my time at University i specialized in structural dynamics in high stress situations, mainly high-rise structures under blast, fire and seismic loads. My final year project involved modeling the results on a departmental software created with Fortran that calculated the deformation of steel framed structures under high temperature loading. My Blog (A.K.A CODEVID-19)

Here lies the temporary home of my soon to be blog, I have never blogged before and as a matter of fact my grammar and punctuation is horrendous so I can only apologies in advance for the extra long sentences and incorrect there’s.

The reason however that I am sat writing the introduction to my own blog, which I have cleverly called CODEVID-19, is because in March 2020 when the world was hit by a global pandemic, I decided to take a leap of faith and change my life for the better.

I decided to (attempt) teach myself web development, something I have wanted to do for a long time but didn’t know exactly where to start or what exactly to learn. I had the luxury of time, I have spent roughly around 40 hours a week doing this for the past 6 months. I felt this was realistic to help maintain a work/life balance, i didn't lock myself in a room and only come out to eat, i did normal stuff, maybe not as much as i would have liked but i wanted to be somewhat strict. I went a couple of road trips, spent time with my partner, the same stuff as I did when I was working. I didn’t want it to consume me but I also didn’t want to fail so the balance was a challenge and it took awhile to find the middle ground.

Others may not have the luxury of time, it is not always realistic to just quit your job and spend 60 hours a week learning something new. However I hope to help provide a path, share my experiences and help ease someone who is in the position I was once in make that change. You might only be able to commit a few hours a week, after work, on your lunch, but hopefully you will gain momentum, avoid those dead ends and learn something new. Even if you don’t end up becoming a web developer you will have gained a valuable skill which cant be ignored.

Web/Software development is a huge field with many possible routes and it can be scary looking at making the change especially with so many unknowns. It certainly was for me 5 months ago but now I’m here, telling you about it and indeed the future is still uncertain but it is honestly the best decision I have ever made and I’m hoping that this story will give someone that nudge they need to do the same.

My journey is still early on, this isn’t one of those unrealistic success stories, this is just me sharing my journey with you and lending a helping hand. When I’ve needed it the most, help has found me in some way shape or form, it’s a kind community of people who have experienced similar things and I hope that I can give back to it and encourage others to be part of it.
